## What Counts as a Business Inquiry
An email is a business inquiry if it:
- Asks about pricing, services, or availability
- Requests a proposal or quote
- Introduces a potential partnership or collaboration opportunity
- Contains a direct question about our offerings
- Is from a potential client reaching out for the first time

The following are NOT business inquiries and should NOT trigger an auto-reply:
- Automated billing or subscription notifications
- Newsletter subscriptions or marketing emails
- Calendar invites or scheduling confirmations
- Emails from known internal contacts
- GitHub, Slack, or other tool notifications

## Reply Tone and Style
- Professional but warm and approachable
- Keep replies concise (3–5 sentences unless more detail is clearly needed)
- Acknowledge the sender's specific question or interest — don't send generic responses
- Always sign off with: [Your Name], [Your Company]
- Do NOT make pricing commitments — invite a call or meeting instead
- If the sender mentions a specific pain point, acknowledge it briefly
